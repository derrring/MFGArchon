- **The FP non-negativity clip silently created mass, and hid the failure it was papering over** (Issue #1671). `fp_fdm_time_stepping.py` raised every negative density to zero with `np.maximum(M, 0)` and a `logger.warning`. Issue #880 added that guard for what it described as *"small negative values (e.g. -1e-15)"* leaking out of a positivity-preserving scheme -- clipping those is mass-neutral -- but the clip was written unbounded, so it also absorbed outright scheme failures. Measured on `Nx=21, Nt=10, T=1.0, FDM_CENTERED`: `divergence_centered` at infinite cell-Péclet (`sigma=0`) drove the density to `-1.577e+04`, each clip added that mass, and after 10 timesteps x 5 Picard iterations the total mass read **6378.77**. Counterfactual, neutering only the clip: mass `0.999999999999`. Worse than the mass error, the clip **manufactured the property the cheap assertions check** -- `min(M)` read `+0.037`, everything finite and non-negative, so `np.isfinite` and `M >= 0` could not distinguish it from a healthy solve. Only mass conservation could, which is why Issue #1673 found that empirically without being able to explain it. The guard now measures the invariant rather than a proxy for it: the clip may fabricate at most `1e-8` of the total mass (round-off from a positivity-preserving scheme sits around `1e-15`; a failed scheme reaches `O(1)`), and beyond that the solve raises with the offending scheme named and the remedy stated. Round-off clipping is unchanged -- of 1862 clip firings across the suite, **1668 are round-off scale and behave exactly as before**. The threshold is deliberately on fabricated mass rather than on `min(M)`: an absolute bound like the old `-1e-10` is meaningless when the density is `O(1e4)`, and two tests whose negatives exceed that bound are correctly left passing because the mass they would fabricate is negligible.
- Marked `test_regime_switching_iterator_runs` as `xfail(strict=True)` against **Issue #1681**, which the new guard surfaced: `RegimeSwitchingIterator` drives `divergence_upwind` -- the positivity-preserving default -- to `-1.0e-03`, fabricating `0.015%` of the mass. It is not a timestep artifact (at `NT=20`, where `dt*sigma^2/dx^2 = 0.45` satisfies the library's own stated bound, the density still reaches `-9.7e-04` and the fabricated fraction is unchanged), so the fixture is left at its original values rather than tuned to hide it.
