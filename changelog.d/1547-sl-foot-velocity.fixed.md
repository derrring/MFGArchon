- **Semi-Lagrangian characteristic feet now read dH/dp from the Hamiltonian** (Issue #1547, RFC #1574). Every foot site hardcoded the quadratic-control-cost velocity `p/lambda` while the Lax-Oleinik value term evaluated the real H through `eval_H_batch` -- a hybrid that solved silently and converged to the wrong limit for any Hamiltonian whose true `dH/dp` is not `p/lambda`. With a congestion factor `c(m)` the foot was too fast by exactly `c(m)` (measured 4x at m=1, 15.96x at a peak-density node, unbounded), and the error against an independent HJB-FDM reference PLATEAUED under refinement (0.0766 -> 0.0788 -> 0.0798) instead of converging. The five sites now route through `eval_dH_dp_batch`, the single-source primitive `HJBFDMSolver` and `HJBGFDMSolver` already used -- SL was the only HJB solver holding a private quadratic copy. Byte-identical for quadratic control costs; the corrected foot converges (0.0109 -> 0.0042 -> 0.0014). The non-smooth DPP path (L1/Bounded) is untouched. `diffusion_method='canonical_cs'` still hardcodes the quadratic running cost on the Lagrangian side and remains congestion-blind -- tracked separately.
