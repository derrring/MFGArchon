- **`SolverResult.mass_conservation_error` was a constant `0.0`, not a measurement** (Issue #1672). The field is documented as `max|integral(m) - 1| over time steps` and defaulted to `0.0`, while **nothing on the coupling path ever wrote it** -- the sole writer in the package is `sinkhorn_solver.py:265`. So every coupled solve reported perfect mass conservation, including the `FDM_CENTERED` case whose mass reached `6378` (Issue #1671). This is why Issue #1673 could not use the field and had to assert mass conservation by hand: it read `0.0` for the divergent solve and for a healthy one alike. The coupling loop now measures it where `M` and the geometry are both in hand, and the default becomes **`None`** -- `0.0` is a valid measurement (a solve that conserved mass exactly), so an unwritten field must not look like one. The cell measure comes from `geometry.volume_element()` rather than a second hand-rolled `dx`: the existing `calculate_total_mass(M, dx)` helper is 1-D only (`np.sum(M * dx, axis=1)`), and adding a second owner for the same quantity is the defect class this repo keeps paying for. A geometry that cannot express a volume element yields `None`, not a fabricated zero. Note for absorbing or Dirichlet walls the deviation is physical outflow rather than an error, and the docstring now says so; giving that case its own semantics is not attempted here.
